The Culture in Pop



Pop music is often defined by its catchy melodies, accessible lyrics, and broad appeal to a wide audience.

By Nehan Sudasinghe

Who's the most popular music artist in the world right now? Check out the cross-platform best-performing artists in February 2023



Here are the artists racking up the most streams and gaining the most new fans across the music world in 2023. As an artists gets more follows and listens on Spotify and Apple Music, Pandora, Deezer, SoundCloud and other streaming platforms, and the better they perform on Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube and across the rest of social media, the closer they get to becoming the biggest artist in the world.








Since the last checked rankings there's been a fair amount of movement amongst the top 10, and some new entries in the top spots. Drake replaces Justin Bieber as the number one best performing artist, as Bieber falls to eighth place. The second most popular artist is Shakira, a new entry riding high off the viral success of a revenge diss track about her ex, which gained 63 million views in 24 hours on YouTube.

















BTS meanwhile have dropped from the top 10 since we last checked in 2022,
despite millions of followers across socials and billions of plays on streaming
services. Miley Cyrus is another newcomer to the list, having released new
song “Flowers” and announced an album is coming in 2023.





Pop music, short for "popular music," is a widely accessible and commercially-oriented genre that has dominated the global music scene for decades. Rooted in the mid-20th century, pop music has continually evolved, shaping and reflecting cultural trends, technological advancements, and social changes. It has become an integral part of modern popular culture, with a massive influence on the tastes and preferences of audiences worldwide.

Characterized by its catchy melodies, rhythmic beats, and relatable lyrics, pop music aims to appeal to a broad audience, transcending boundaries of age, gender, and ethnicity. It often incorporates elements from various musical styles, blending genres such as rock, R&B, hip-hop, electronic, and more, making it diverse and adaptable.
